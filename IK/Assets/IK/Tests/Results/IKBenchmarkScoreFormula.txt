IK Benchmark Composite Score

Normalization
For higher-is-better metrics: N_high(x) = (x - x_min) / (x_max - x_min + eps)
For lower-is-better metrics: N_low(x) = (x_max - x) / (x_max - x_min + eps)
The min and max are computed over all tested (solver, step_scale) points in this benchmark.

Score Formula
Score = 100 * (
  0.20 * N_high(reachable_success_rate)
  + 0.10 * N_low(reachable_avg_success_iterations)
  + 0.10 * N_low(reachable_avg_runtime_ms)
  + 0.10 * N_low(reachable_avg_final_error)
  + 0.20 * N_low(reachable_avg_joint_rotation_deviation_deg)
  + 0.05 * N_low(unreachable_avg_runtime_ms)
  + 0.15 * N_low(unreachable_avg_final_error)
  + 0.10 * N_low(unreachable_avg_joint_rotation_deviation_deg)
)

Interpretation
Higher scores are better.
The final bar chart uses the best score over the tested step-scale range for each solver.
